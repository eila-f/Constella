Constella plans to launch four satellites. These would be placed at an altitude of around 42,000 miles, and would orbit in 48-hour periods in order to create a signal that can stretch over the entire planet. This would allow for coverage over 95% of the world once we take advantage of a > 5º elevation angle and strategical placement of outage regions .

Current satellites orbit in elliptical shapes, but recent advancements have found that more circular paths are able to harness nonlinear pertubation forces, saving more fuel, and becoming more cost-efficient. Additionally, we plan to operate the satellites at nearly-polar inclinations, creating less path deviations and more fuel saved.

Some other companies are also creating constellation satellites. However, due to our usage of new research to save on fuel and ultimately launch and maintenance costs, we believe that our company would be the cheapest out of them all. This would help those currently without high-speed internet access able recieve a connection to the world in an instant.
